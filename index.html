Our Mission

Oregon Harbor of Hope is dedicated to empowering those impacted by homelessness, social marginalization, and life-altering challenges. We work collaboratively to deliver solutions that restore hope, build resilience, and foster lasting change across our communities.

  • We are committed to empowering individuals whose life challenges have resulted in them living on the streets by providing guidance, promoting personal connections, driving collaboration, and delivering impactful solutions. Through this work, we strive to restore individual hope and dignity, foster healing, build resilience and create lasting change for our community.
  • We are dedicated to supporting our homeless neighbors in gaining access to safe, humane places to dwell and the essential services they need, not just to survive, but to reach stability and achieve long term well being.
  • Recognizing that every individual’s journey is unique, we address the complex intersections of mental health, substance use, criminal justice involvement, and poverty with compassion, innovation, and strategic partnerships.

Our Approach

Identify

Identify effective programs that successfully reduce homelessness and its root causes.

Incubate

Incubate promising initiatives, transforming them into self-sustaining nonprofits.

Initiate

Initiate collaboration among nonprofits, community leaders, influencers, volunteers, and citizens dedicated to addressing homelessness, substance abuse, criminality, and poverty.

Inform

Inform the public, private, and nonprofit sectors about the nature of comprehensive approaches to successfully ending homelessness, empowering them to take meaningful action.

Who We Are

We create and support programs that meet people where they are—offering real help, compassionate care, and practical tools for rebuilding lives. From housing support to community partnerships, everything we offer is designed to foster stability, dignity, and long-term change.

Programs

Our fundraising goals are ambitious because meeting the needs of our unhoused neighbors is so critical. We're currently fundraising for the following programs and services:

Better Neighbors is a nonprofit organization dedicated to providing programming and educational opportunities to marginalized populations, especially those affected by incarceration and addiction disorders. We believe that everyone deserves a chance to rebuild their lives and become positive, contributing members of their communities.

Art Inside Out uses art as a vehicle to connect us to positive information and guidance and will reinforce our value and create a better you. AIO is an evidence-based program that provides a positive and creative atmosphere. AIO can help make that needed connection for change.

Founded in 2021, HomeShare Oregon is working hard to change the cultural norm around homesharing. We see homesharing as an immediate solution to our affordable housing crisis. Connecting home providers who have spare space to share with renters seeking affordable housing is a common-sense and immediately available response to prevent housing instability.

On January 1, 2024, management of the River District Navigation Center at 111 NW Naito Pkwy transitioned to the City of Portland. The Navigation Center was previously managed by the Oregon Harbor of Hope nonprofit, who oversaw the location under a sub-lease that expired at the end of 2023. To prevent the 90 individuals staying at the Navigation Center from potentially returning to living unsheltered in Portland, Mayor Wheeler directed the City to step in and maintain the location as a shelter location.
